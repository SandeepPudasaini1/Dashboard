What is CoC?
Continuum of Care
▼

A Continuum of Care (CoC) is a group including homeless service providers, governmental entities, businesses, community advocates, public housing agencies, school districts, mental health agencies, hospitals, universities, affordable housing developers, law enforcement, and persons with lived experience of homelessness.

CoCs carry out the responsibilities required to receive federal funding to support the homelessness response system.

🧭
Mission
What we are working toward
▼

The mission of CoCs is to support a community-wide commitment to ending homelessness, quickly re-house people, improve access to mainstream benefits, and increase self-sufficiency among those experiencing homelessness.

CoCs work through a coordinated, strategic, and evidence-based approach to assist people experiencing or at risk of homelessness.

🎯

Functional Zero

Functional Zero is reached when a community shows that its homeless service system has practically ended homelessness for a population in a way that is measurable and equitable, prevents homelessness whenever possible, and ensures that when homelessness does occur, it is rare, brief, and a one-time occurrence.

Measurable Equitable Rare Brief One-time occurrence

What are our project types?
Program types in the response system
▼
🤝
Street Outreach
Supports individuals experiencing homelessness by meeting them where they are, building trust, addressing basic needs, and connecting them to services and housing support.
🏠
Emergency Shelter
Provides a safe temporary place to stay while helping individuals and families address immediate needs and work toward more stable housing.
🏘
Transitional Housing
Offers temporary housing and supportive services to help individuals and families move toward permanent and affordable housing.
⚡
Rapid Rehousing
Helps households quickly exit homelessness and secure permanent housing through short-term rental assistance and supportive services.
🔗
Joint TH-RRH
Combines transitional housing and rapid rehousing to provide a safe place to stay and support the move into permanent housing.
🏡
Permanent Supportive Housing
Provides permanent housing with long-term assistance and supportive services for households with at least one member with a disability.

What are the homelessness categories?
How homelessness is defined
▼

The federal government defines homelessness in several categories to reflect different experiences and needs. These categories help guide funding, services, and response strategies.

Literally Homeless
An individual or family without a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence. This includes people who:
  • Are staying in a place not meant for human habitation
  • Are staying in emergency shelter or temporary shelter
  • Are leaving an institution after a short stay and were homeless before entering
Imminent Risk of Homelessness
An individual or family who is at immediate risk of losing their housing and does not have other safe housing options.
  • Will lose housing within 14 days
  • No new housing has been identified
  • Lacks support or resources to secure housing
Fleeing Domestic Violence
Includes individuals or families fleeing or attempting to flee domestic violence who do not have another safe residence or the resources to obtain one.

How do we collect this data?
